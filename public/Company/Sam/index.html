Scrapbook Memoir

About me

Midland, TX

Born to father who immigrated from Palestine in the 60s and mother of German and English descent.

Opportunity

Dad left his family and did not speak English when he came. Many strangers and friends helped him throughout his journey. He graduated from Texas Tech University and became a Professional Petroleum Engineer. My parents married in Hobbs, NM.

Growing Up

Raised in small town New Mexico. Dear are the Family, Work ethic, Education values and friendships it offered.

Technology

Cannot remember a time without fascination for computers. We were encouraged, "If you copy the program perfectly, then it will play a song." We were so proud when it sang, which was rare.

First Crush

Years after first meeting my playground girlfriend our journeys crossed paths once again, and she became my wife. The Land of Enchantment, a great place to start a journey.

Sisters

Learned a lot growing up with a twin sister and an older sister. We were taught to respect everyone and be kind. Don't be mean. Faulty still, we each carry a strong moral compass.

High School

Lived in Carlsbad, NM from 4th Grade through H.S. Played tennis, roller hockey, soccer, and Supercomputing Challenge of course. All activities that offered a special medium to get to know new people throughout my journey.

New Mexico State University

Money was tight but thankfully NM did not charge for applying to its universities and offered tuition scholarships. Applied only to NMSU and started the Electrical Engineering program to learn how computers work, finally.

Programming

Programming came naturally and it was fun. Many partners excelled at hardware while I at the simulators and software. Ended up specializing in real-time digital signal processing and wrote MATLAB compression algorithms and DSP assembly code for final project.

IBM, Austin TX

College and engineering provided so many great opportunities. One of those was intern and co-op work at IBM. Spent two semesters and a summer working there with processor design verification and software teams.

Study Abroad, Malta

Thirst for seeing the world led to a semester of study abroad in the island country of Malta. A hundred students from all over the world converged for a college experience of a lifetime. Quickly learned about new cultures, adapting, and self.

Report Card

Over time, the classroom became a challenge that was fun to succeed at. Diverse experiences including working at the IT and UNIX admin department throughout, strong foundation, and supportive peer groups were key ingredients to the college experience.

Electrical Engineer. Graduation.

Graduation was an eager time, the world was opened up. Always dreamed about excelling at a career. Accepted the first job offer that came my way from a company called Harris Corporation. Near family starting out in my college town of Las Cruces, NM.

New Professional

Worked for Harris for 10 years, from the East to West coast, and even internationally. Driven to help my teams achieve success and absorb as many lessons and new situations as possible.

Lots of Moving

There were many moves and different cities in life after college. Many experiences, many lessons, many tools.

University of Florida

Passion for developing technology and quickly learned how instrumental business acumen was to exactly that. Business determines what gets developed, how, who, why. Business shoulders the results. Jumped at the business program paid for by my company.

Found My Wife, again

In the midst of Coast to Coast travels, attended a friend's wedding back home in Carlsbad, NM and met Nikki once again. Our journeys crossed paths unimaginably, and I knew she would be my wife.

MBA

My future wife was with me in December 2008 at graduation from the University of Florida. A great celebration for a cohort that stuck together for 27 months.

I Do

Living and working in Silicon Valley when she said 'Yes.' It's a most vivid memory. Later that year we married in our hometown of Carlsbad, NM.

Moving Abroad

After helping out with acquisition work near corporate headquarters in Florida, my wife and I went on a two year expat assignment in Abu Dhabi, UAE.

An International Experience

A lifetime experience to live and do business in an international country on the move. My wife was so strong and provided so much support during a whirlwind of change. She had the great opportunity of providing valuable Speech Therapy services while working for an Emirate organization and with children from all over the world.

Baby

A year after living overseas, we were thrilled to find out Baby was coming. We traveled home so Bay could be born in our home town, Carlsbad, USA.

Back Home

Returned overseas for a couple of months to finish up work after Bay was born. Our new baby drawing us closer home along with career aspirations of technology productization, I made the decision to leave the company I had been with for 10 years.

Transition Reset

It was a great run with a boutique defense company, but a transition was in order. No better place to reset than our hometown and with family. Accepted Project Management offer in town's largest industry.

Career Playbook

Got into the habit of capturing career aspirations and roadmap. This is the playbook captured when opting for a transition. Mine is a palpable passion for innovative business and technology commercialization.

Twiqed, 3 Years

For almost three years, friends and fellow software engineers and I worked on launching a new online marketplace. It was a great entrepreneurial foray for all of us, and provided valuable experience.

Startup

Founding a company with discretionary effort from a team with day jobs is a unique and special experience. Learned to do (or at least try) everything, and be able to clearly articulate your message, execution, and end game.

Entrepreneurial Spirit

We built the prototype, we signed up Users, we pitched to investors. We had a decision to make; go all in or stop.
